My Story

I am Kolawole Abdullahi, a 200 level Health Information Management student and someone who takes the work of managing health data very personally. I grew up watching people in my community struggle with a healthcare system that seemed to lose track of them every time they visited a new hospital. A missing file here, a repeated test there, a wrong diagnosis because a doctor did not have the full picture.

Those experiences planted a question in my mind that never went away: why is it so hard to keep track of something as important as a person's health history? That question led me to Health Information Management and I have not looked back since.

My goal is straightforward. I want to help build health systems where data flows correctly, records are protected properly and every decision is backed by accurate information. I may only be in my second year but I am approaching this with the seriousness it deserves.
